Gatlinburg

The Millhouse Kitchen

Set inside a restored 1940s grist mill overlooking a mountain stream, The Millhouse Kitchen is Gatlinburg's standout farm-to-table experience. The winter menu leans into Appalachian comfort classics — smoked trout chowder, cast iron cornbread, and house-cured country ham biscuits. Reservations strongly recommended on weekends.

Pigeon Forge

Forge & Ember Smokehouse

A wood-fired barbecue joint with serious East Tennessee roots. Pitmaster Ray Conner has been slow-smoking brisket, pulled pork, and St. Louis ribs here since 2009. The sides — particularly the white cheddar mac and smoked baked beans — are as good as the meat. Counter service, family-friendly, no wait on weekday lunches.

Sevierville

Creekside Cafe & Bakery

The kind of breakfast spot locals have been protective of for years. Creekside opens at 6 a.m. and runs until early afternoon, serving house-made biscuits, country gravy, locally-sourced eggs, and fresh-baked pies that rotate daily. Cash and card accepted. Expect a short wait on Saturday mornings — worth every minute.

Gatlinburg

Ridgeline Bistro

A small-plates and craft cocktail concept on the edge of the Arts & Crafts Community, Ridgeline Bistro brings a more refined dining option to the Gatlinburg area. The menu changes seasonally and emphasizes locally sourced produce and proteins. A quiet, unhurried atmosphere makes it ideal for a date night away from the Strip.
